## What Went Well

- The team delivered broad Epic 2 operator capabilities, but with a critical coherence gap: canonical configuration authority was not consistently enforced across all seed/runtime persistence paths.
- Deterministic behavior was treated as a first-class quality attribute (diagnostics grouping/stability, immutable publish/evidence handling, deterministic route and state behavior).
- Story-level verification discipline was strong, with targeted suites across web, API, scripts, and selected browser checks.
- Runtime-boundary discipline held consistently in UX and behavior: controls were visible for discoverability while execution remained disabled until Epic 3.
- Canonical authority guidance materially improved during the epic via architecture docs and integration-level assertions.

## Challenges and Pain Points

- Canonical-authority drift remained in active seed/runtime paths: extension payload (`definition_extensions_json`) still carried canonical-like configuration in at least one story seeding path.
- Governance was partially fragmented: canonical policy matured quickly, but not all story fixtures/seeding paths were aligned at the same pace.
- Complexity expanded rapidly in methodology/fact/workflow authoring surfaces, increasing coordination load between contract, UI, and seed data owners.
- Some global repo verification friction remained outside story scope, reducing confidence in fully clean cross-repo signals during story closeout.

## Key Insights

- Canonical authority is not just a schema decision; it must be enforced in seeds, projections, fixtures, and tests at the same time.
- "Policy present" is not equal to "path safe". Every operational path must be checked for fallback behavior and hidden authority drift.
- The Epic 2 boundary discipline was effective and should be reused in Epic 3: explicit deferment language prevented accidental runtime coupling.
