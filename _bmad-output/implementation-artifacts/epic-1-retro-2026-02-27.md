## What Went Well

- Test-driven development discipline stayed strong and improved implementation reliability.
- Agent instruction adherence was consistently good across stories.
- Effect-first usage improved over the epic; by Stories 1.4 and 1.5 the implementation flow adopted Effect patterns with less explicit prompting.
- Implementation quality was mostly strong; most late-cycle fixes were precision hardening rather than structural rewrites.
- Deterministic diagnostics, append-only evidence, and transactional no-partial-write guarantees were repeatedly reinforced.

## Challenges and Friction

- Progressive schema and field expansion created temporary authority ambiguity.
- `definitionJson` authority drift persisted too long after normalized schemas/tables were introduced.
- Early stories overused `try/catch` in places better served by typed Effect error channels.
- Cleanup/hardening cycles were needed to remove legacy authority paths and tighten deterministic behavior.

## Key Insights

- Incremental delivery worked, but promotion rules must be explicit: once behavior becomes contract-critical, move it to normalized schema and stop reading it from extension payloads.
- Keeping a catchall payload is useful for emerging fields, but only when non-authoritative boundaries are enforced.
- TDD plus deterministic diagnostics contracts reduced regression risk and sped up late-story hardening.

## Clarified Team Agreement on `definitionJson` / `definitionExtensions`

- Allowed: catchall storage for fluid/new/experimental fields that are not yet fully structured.
- Not allowed: using catchall payload fields as canonical authority for execution decisions, eligibility, publish authority, or deterministic contract behavior.
- Promotion rule: once a field becomes contract-critical, promote to normalized schema/contracts and treat extension payload as non-authoritative metadata.
